This policy outlines the guidelines and expectations for the usage of penetration testing tools within Athena OS.

1. Legal and Ethical Use:
    <ol type="a">
       <li>Penetration testing activities using the tools provided by Athena OS must be conducted in compliance with all applicable laws, regulations, and ethical standards.</li>
       <li>Obtain proper authorization and permission before performing penetration testing activities on any system or network. Ensure you have the legal rights and consent to conduct testing.</li>
    </ol>

2. Responsible Usage:
    <ol type="a">
       <li>Use penetration testing tools responsibly, ensuring that testing activities are limited to authorized systems and networks.</li>
       <li>Exercise caution and avoid causing disruption, damage, or harm to systems or networks being tested.</li>
       <li>Respect the privacy and confidentiality of individuals and organizations during penetration testing activities. Do not access or disclose sensitive information without proper authorization.</li>
    </ol>

3. Reporting Vulnerabilities:
    <ol type="a">
       <li>If you discover vulnerabilities or security issues during your testing activities, follow responsible disclosure practices.</li>
       <li>Report vulnerabilities to the appropriate parties or maintainers, allowing them sufficient time to address the issues before disclosing them publicly.</li>
    </ol>

4. Documentation and Usage Guidelines:
    <ol type="a">
       <li>Familiarize yourself with the documentation and usage guidelines provided for each penetration testing tool included in Athena OS.</li>
       <li>Understand the proper usage, capabilities, and limitations of each tool to ensure accurate and effective testing.</li>
       <li>Keep up-to-date with the latest information and advisories regarding the tools in order to use them effectively and responsibly.</li>
    </ol>

5. Community Support:
    <ol type="a">
       <li>Engage with the Athena OS community and relevant security communities to exchange knowledge, seek support, and share insights related to penetration testing and security research.</li>
       <li>Participate in discussions, forums, and mailing lists to learn from experienced users and contribute back to the community.</li>
    </ol>
    
By using Athena OS and its penetration testing tools, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Penetration Test Tools Policy and agree to comply with its terms and conditions.
